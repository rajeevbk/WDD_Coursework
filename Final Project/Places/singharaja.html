Singharaja Forest Reserve

Introduction and About

Sinharaja meaning 'Lion Kingdom' is a rainforest designated in 1989 as a UNESCO Natural World Heritage Site and it is one of the most important biodiversity hotspots in Sri lanka bursting with various species of flora and fauna an inevitable treasure trove
There are so many factors that highlight the importance of this tropical forest reserve in terms of its exceptional biodiversity.
This lowland evergreen rain forest is home to 95% of the endemic birds of Sri Lanka! and believed to be home for almost 50% of Sri Lankas endemic wildlife drawing thousands of visitors wanting to explore & experience this natural wonder that is rich in endemism steeped in deep legend and mystery.
'Sinharaja' translates to Lion(Sinha) & King(Raja) alluding to a popular belief according to which the origin of the Sinhala people is from the union between a princess and the lion king who once lived in this forest. Situated 172.1 km from Colombo via the Southern Expressway this forest is accessible from 3 entrances & though smaller than other National Parks,Singharaja extends over an area of 11,187 hectares.

Vegetation and Rainfall

Tropical humid wet evergreen forest with a high degree of endemism, is said to be the vegetation at Sinharaja and a striking characteristic of the forest is the loftiness of the dominant trees growing to heights of 35m to 40m or more with unbelievable genetic potential. The untapped genetic potential of Sinharaja flora is enormous. Of the 211 woody trees and lianas, 66% or 139 of them are endemic to the forest reserve. The lower plants such as Ferns and epiphytes of the 25 endemic species 13 have been recorded within Sinharaja itself. Some families such as Dipterocarpaceae show an endemism more than 90%. Similarly, high levels of endemism are visible for the lower plants like ferns, Epiphyts as well. Out of 25 general endemic to Sri Lanka 13 are represented in Sinharaja.
Inclusive of trees, shrubs, herbs and seedlings the total vegetation density, at this beautiful forest reserve has been estimated to be around 240,000 individuals per hectare.
2 well known & important rivers in Sri lanka namely the Gin and Kalu Ganga(Rivers) and many other waterways are fed and nourished by the waters that flow from this forest reserve.

Mammals

Singharaja is a favorable sanctuary for various species of mammals, reptiles, insects, fish, amphibians,birds and plant life; all co-existing within the ecosystem. Speaking about the smaller mammals Porcupines, Otters, three types of squirrels, two species of Mongoose- , two species of Civets; Ring-tailed and Golden Palm, many species of Bandicoot, Rats, Bats and even the very rare Pangolin are to name a prominent few and now moving on to the Larger Mammals, Elephants, Sambar, Leopards, Fishing Cat, Rusty Spotted Cat, Barking deer, Mouse deer, Jackal and Wild boar and a reddish brown monkey are a few of the many amazing species you will come across at Singharaja.

Birds at Singharaja

Sinharaja has recorded over 154 species of birds and is known to have a mixed-species feeding flocks or informally called a bird wave where two or more species feed and move together. This unique sight witnessed at Sinharaja sometimes consists of flocks containing 30-50 birds of various species sometimes 10-12 species, erupting in various bird sounds as they feed and move within a short time. Taking lead in this exercise is the Orange-billed babbler and the Crested drongo.
The endemic birds witnessed here include Ceylon Lorikeet, Layards parakeet, Jungle & Spur fowl, Sri Lanka Blue magpie, Sri Lanka Grackle, Ceylon Hanging parrot, Ceylon Grey hornbill, Ashy-headed laughing thrush,Spotted-wing thrush, Brown-capped babbler, Ceylon Hill mynah, Red faced malkoha, Legges flower-pecker amongst many others.
