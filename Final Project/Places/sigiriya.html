Welcome to Sigirya!

Sigiriya, the lore.

Sigirya also knon as the "Lions Rock" is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Sri Lanka with significant archeological and historical importance.

It is an ancient fotress built by King Kashyapa, on a huge standing rock more than 4000 years ago. King Kashyapa built the palace to seek refuge from his borhter Maggallana who was the legal heir to the throne and was threatning his life

The palace â€“ Sigiriya was constructed using the most advanced technologies of the time and was richly decorated with colorful frescos, gardens and water parks all on top of a rock.

This archeological masterpiece is one of the most sort out tourist attractions in Sri Lanka and is a must see for anyone visiting the country.

What to expect when visiting Sigiriya

The Lions Claw entrance

The main entrance to the palace, located in the nothern side of the rock was designed in the form of a huge stone lion standing boldy to welcome anyone coming through the entance with pride. Eventhough the upper torso of the Lion has been destroyed the lion paws remain to this day showcasing the ingenuity in design showcased by the early Sri Lankans.

The Mirror wall

The mirror wall in Sigirya is one of the most striking features of the fotress. It is said that the wall was so polished that the King could see his reflection on it, hence the name "Mirror Wall". The wall is covered with various paintings and inscriptions that are dated from the 8th Century. These inscriptions are proof that Sigiriya was a tourist destination even thousands of years ago.

Sigirya ancient Pool

The ancient water fountains of Sigiriya are to this day considered as an architectrual marvel considering how water was carried hundreds of metres up the rock with very primitive technology. They are located in the western part of the rock and are with a complex hydraulic system, which consists of canals, locks, lakes, dams, bridges, fountains, as well as surface and underground water pumps.
